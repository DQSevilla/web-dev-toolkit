Basic Project Management

What is Project Management?

  • The PMI (Project Management Institute) defined a project as "A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product or service."
  • A project is a specific, finite task which typically requires resources from various areas, has a primary customer or sponsor and involves uncertainty.

Project Management Lifecycle

  • Advantages of formal project management:
    • Better resource control
    • Improved customer relations
    • Shorter developement times
    • Lower costs, higher profit margins
    • High quality, readability and productivity
    • Better internal coordination and morale
  • Project Managers:
    • Deals with planning, procurment and execution of a project.
    • Expected to coordinate all activities needed to reach the project's goal.
    • Responsible to:
      • The client and the environment
      • Identify and correct problems early
      • Make timely decisions about trade-offs and conflicting goals
      • Ensure that managers of seperate tasks do not preamaturely optimize
        their task performance at the expense of the overall project
  • Project Lifecycles:
    • There are many models, but the most basic (covered here) is the Waterfall Model
    • Typical steps in CS projects include: Inception, Requirements and Analysis, Design
      Implementation, Testing, Debugging, Distribution, and Maintenance
    • The "Vital Dozen" for Project Managers
