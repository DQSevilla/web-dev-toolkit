Networking Fundamentals

Below is a detailed introduction to the fundamentals of networking.

Networking

What is Networking?

  • A Network can be defined as two or more computers connected together such that they can share resources.
    The connection can be phyisical, through wires and cables, or locgical via the transport of data accross the physical media.
  • A resource may be a file, folder, printer, disk, drive, etc.
  • Pros of Networking:
    • Enables faster communication between parties
    • Encourages the use of standard policies and procedures
    • Provides backup and recovery support for our data (i.e. clouds)
    • Easy to store coppies of our data
    • Shared resources lead to inexpensive communication
  • Cons of Networking:
    • Hardware, software and set-up costs
    • Management and administration costs
    • Undesirable sharing or illegal behavior

Types of Networks

  • Local Area Network (LAN): Covers a small geographic area
  • Metropolitan Area Network (MAN): Covers a larger (city-size) geographic area
  • Wide Area Network (WAN): Covers a very large area (ex. Internet)

  • Intranet: Private enterprise network. Internet: Worldwide network of networks
